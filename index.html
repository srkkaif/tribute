Tribute to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the "Missile Man of India," was the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he was a brilliant scientist and a great visionary who played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Dr. Kalam was not just a renowned scientist but also a beloved teacher, an author, and a visionary leader. He worked tirelessly to make India self-reliant in defense technologies and inspired millions of young minds to dream big and achieve their goals. He authored several books, including the famous "Wings of Fire," which chronicles his early life and work in Indian space research.

Achievements

  • Project Director of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.
  • Played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, making India a nuclear-armed state.
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.
  • Authored many books, including "Wings of Fire," "India 2020," and "Ignited Minds."

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His legacy continues to inspire millions of people across the world, especially the youth of India, to pursue knowledge, creativity, and excellence.
